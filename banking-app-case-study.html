Ordra

All your orders, in one place

Project Overview

Project: Ordra — order tracking app concept • Type: Mobile app

Role: Solo designer • Duration: 1.5 weeks • Tools: Figma, Notion

Ordra aggregates purchases from multiple e‑commerce platforms using secure email parsing, giving people and small businesses a single, clear view of every order, shipment, and delivery.

Problem

Online shoppers and operators managing multiple storefronts struggle to track purchases and deliveries spread across emails and retailer portals. This costs time, creates anxiety, and leads to missed deliveries or returns.

How might we give users a single, reliable source of truth for all orders regardless of the store?

Research

Methods: 5 quick interviews, competitive scan (Shop, Arrive, Parcel), inbox walkthroughs.

Key insights:

  • People search inboxes multiple times to find tracking links and delivery dates.
  • Shipment status language varies by retailer, creating confusion.
  • Small businesses want a simple way to filter orders by status and vendor.

Final Design

Key screens that define the experience.

Impact

Prototype Testing Results

Tested with 8 users who manage multiple online orders. Users reported an estimated 5-7 minutes saved per order lookup compared to searching emails and logging into multiple retailer accounts.

Time Savings Scenario

For a user tracking 20 orders per month across Amazon, Target, and other retailers, Ordra could save approximately 2 hours per month that would otherwise be spent searching emails, logging into accounts, and navigating tracking pages.

Market Opportunity

With over 268 million e-commerce customers in the US alone, capturing just 10% market share at $9/month would generate $2.4 billion in annual recurring revenue. Small businesses managing inventory across platforms represent an additional premium tier opportunity.
